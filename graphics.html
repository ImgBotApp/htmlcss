Graphics

Drawing was the first activity I recall being passionate about as it enabled me to block out a world that could get confusing for a clueless kid. When I discovered video games, I likely spent equal time playing as I did drawing cartoons based on them. In providing illustrations for Energraft, I committed to adhering to the codebase-inherited practice of rendering all sprites pixel perfect. I began creating symbols when I was compelled by the realization of shallowness to impart a sense of depth past the initial impression.

Illustrations

I created sprites for a video game (in-game screens), which are interspersed throughout the following directory along with other game art: github.com/rjbx/2dgame-demo/tree/master/core/assets/images

Icons

I attempted to incorporate into an icon all or parts of the typography of an ability (move; climb; wall jump; dash; hover) of a video game character, as well as, in the initial two samples, the form of a body lifting an object, and a ladder, respectively.

Logos

I attempted to incorporate all or parts of the typography of "Qualiv" into a logo, as well as, respectively, the form of a bird (top-down-body as well as head-side), an olive, a face, and legs.
